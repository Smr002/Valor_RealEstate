Homebuyer Opportunities Increase as Inventory Rises, Says National Association of Realtors

Home / Blog

 In a potential boon for homebuyers, the National Association of Realtors (NAR) is reporting a rise in housing inventory across the United States. This comes after a period of historically low inventory that has frustrated buyers and fueled bidding wars.
 According to the NAR, existing-home sales have shown a slight increase recently, breaking a streak of declines and signaling a potential shift in the real estate market for 2024. The key factor appears to be a rise in the number of homes available for purchase.
  "Rising inventory and moderating price conditions are bringing buyers back to the market," said Lawrence Yun, NAR's chief economist, in a statement. "Affordability, however, remains challenging as home price gains are roughly three times wage growth."
 The increase in inventory can be attributed to several factors. With mortgage rates hovering near 7%, some potential buyers have been priced out of the market, leading to a slowdown in demand. Additionally, some homeowners who locked in ultra-low mortgage rates in recent years may be reluctant to sell, but a gradual rise in new construction is also adding to overall inventory.
  While this is positive news for homebuyers, affordability concerns remain. The median price for existing homes continues to rise, though the pace of appreciation has slowed compared to earlier in 2023. This means that even with more homes on the market, some buyers may still struggle to compete due to rising prices and mortgage rates.
  Analysts predict a more balanced market in the coming months, with buyers having more options and potentially less competition. However, the long-term outlook for housing affordability remains uncertain.
