Pending home sales fell 2.1% in May

Home / Blog

The housing market is showing signs of a cooldown, with a recent report indicating a decline in buyer activity. According to the National Association of Realtors (NAR), pending home sales, a forward-looking indicator based on signed contracts, fell 2.1% in May compared to April.
  This decrease comes amidst rising mortgage rates, which have impacted affordability for many potential buyers. The report highlights a mixed regional picture, with the Northeast and West experiencing slight increases in pending sales, while the South and Midwest saw larger declines.
 "The market is at an interesting point with rising inventory and lower demand," said NAR Chief Economist Lawrence Yun. "This trend is likely to continue in the coming months, giving buyers more options and potentially leading to a more balanced market."
 While a decrease in pending sales suggests a potential slowdown in closed sales in the coming months, it's important to note the context. Year-over-year, pending home sales are still down 6.6%, indicating that activity remains elevated compared to pre-pandemic levels.
 Experts believe this trend could benefit buyers who have faced intense competition and bidding wars in recent years. A more balanced market could provide them with more negotiating power and a wider selection of homes.
 However, potential buyers should remain cautious. While affordability concerns may ease slightly, overall housing inventory is still tight in many areas. Additionally, the impact of rising interest rates on the broader economy remains uncertain.
 For those considering entering the market, staying informed about local market conditions and working with a qualified realtor is crucial. They can help navigate the changing landscape and identify opportunities that align with individual needs and budgets.
